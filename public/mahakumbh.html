Maha Kumbh Itinerary

Overview

Maha Kumbh is the world's largest spiritual gathering, held once every 12 years at the confluence of the Ganges, Yamuna, and the mythical Saraswati rivers. Millions of devotees, saints, and tourists come together to take a holy dip, which is believed to cleanse one's sins and lead to salvation. The festival is known for its grand processions, where Naga Sadhus (ascetics) and spiritual leaders march with chants and religious fervor. Apart from its religious significance, Maha Kumbh is also a cultural and intellectual hub, featuring spiritual discourses, Vedic teachings, devotional music, and artistic performances. The fair offers a unique blend of tradition, devotion, and celebration, making it a once-in-a-lifetime experience.

Day-wise Plan

Day 1: Arrival & local sightseeing in Prayagraj.
Day 2: Take a holy dip in the Ganges & explore Akshayavat.
Day 3: Attend cultural performances & spiritual discourses.
